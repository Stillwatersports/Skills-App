The Story

Born on
the water.

It all started for me at Hockey Opportunity Camp in South River, Ontario. I was six years old my first day of summer camp… I'm not sure if it was seeing the waterski coaches make a human pyramid, or sliding across the water on their bare feet… But I was hooked.

Over the next decade and a half I would return to HOC as a camper, I wanted nothing more than to be a waterski coach. I went through Leader in Training and Counsellor in Training programs, National lifeguard certification, Canadian Waterski coaches certification and eventually spent three seasons as a waterski coach — passing the handle on to hundreds, if not thousands of kids.

Today I compete, coach, and volunteer out of Ottawa, specializing in all three barefoot disciplines — slalom, trick, and jump. I train year-round and rank within the top 100 worldwide barefoot athletes. I am competitive by nature, but nothing is more rewarding than teaching someone to waterski for the first time. Today I also serve as a volunteer at the national sport organization level, growing the sport of waterskiing through fundraising, organizing tournaments and promoting watersports in Canada.
