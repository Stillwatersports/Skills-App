Session Day

We come to your lake.

On the day of your session We arrive at your lake with everything — the Sanger DLX boat, all equipment, life jackets, and a structured plan ready to go. You don't need to bring a single thing except yourselves.

What to bring: Swimwear, sunscreen, water, snacks, and a towel. Everything else is provided.

05

Learn, progress,
have fun.

Every session follows a structured progression — starting with safety and fundamentals, building through skills at the right pace for each rider. Our progressive system is designed so that everyone gets up on their first session.

After your session, all Stillwater Sports students get access to the Stillwater Skills app — track your progress, earn Bronze through Platinum badges, and pick up exactly where you left off next time, or submit your videos in the app for approvals of a new skill!

Common Questions

Before you book.

Do I need my own boat or lake access?
No! No boat required, we bring our own. Typically we got to our clients cottage however we can arrange to meet at a public park on a lake near you, or come to us. We also run on the Rideau River in Ottawa. Distance surcharges apply for locations 1.5+ hrs from Manotick.
What age can kids start?
Kids of all ages are welcome. We have equipment for all sizes. Our youngest skier to date was 2.5 years old! There must be an adult present on shore at all times, waivers are require for all minors. If a minors parents are not present additional consent is required from the parents.
What if the weather is bad?
Light rain — we go. Heavy rain or thunder — we reschedule with full credit. Full refund if no date is available before September 30.
What is the deposit policy?
30% deposit required. Non-refundable. Full payment due 48 hours before session via Square payment link. See our refund policy for full details.
Can we use our own equipment?
Yes! We have all the gear including lifejackets however a properly fitting and comfortable lifejacket makes a huge difference. If you have a favourite lifejacket, ski, or handle, we encourage you to use it!
