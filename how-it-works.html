01
First Step
Request a date online.

Head to the booking page, pick an open date on the live calendar, and fill in your details — your name, lake or cottage area, group size, sport, and skill level. The whole thing takes about 2 minutes.

No phone calls needed. No back-and-forth emails. Just submit your request and we take it from there.

What you'll need: Your preferred date, the name of your lake or cottage area, approximate group size, and a rough idea of what you want to try. Don't worry if you're not sure on the sport — just say so and we'll figure it out together.

02
Within 24 Hours
Christian confirms and plans your session.

Within 24 hours Christian will reach out to confirm the date, discuss your group's goals, choose the best spot on your lake, and tailor a session plan around your skill levels and interests.

Once confirmed, a 30% deposit link is sent via Square to secure your booking. Full payment is due 48 hours before your session.

What to expect: A quick message or call to confirm logistics — which part of the lake works best, what time works for your group, and any specific goals or questions. Christian will also confirm equipment sizes if needed.
03
Session Day
We come to your lake. Fully set up.

On the day of your session Christian arrives at your lake with everything — the Sanger DLX boat, all equipment, life jackets, and a structured plan ready to go. You don't need to bring a single thing except yourselves.

Sessions run AM or PM for half days, or a full 7 hours on the water for full days with a 1-hour midday break. Weather is monitored the night before — you'll be notified early if conditions require a reschedule.

What to bring: Swimwear, sunscreen, water, snacks, and a towel. Wetsuits, PFDs, all boards, skis, ropes, and handles are provided. Wear athletic swimwear that fits securely — nothing that'll come off on a fall.
04
On the Water
Learn, progress, and have a great time.

Every session follows a structured progression — starting with safety and fundamentals, building through skills at the right pace for each rider. No one gets left behind, and no one gets bored waiting.

Our progressive system is designed so that everyone gets up on their first session. Kids and complete beginners especially — the boom, the right equipment, and proper coaching makes all the difference.

After your session: All Stillwater Sports students get access to the Stillwater Skills app — track your progress, earn Bronze through Platinum badges, and pick up exactly where you left off next time.
Common Questions

Before you book.

Do I need my own boat or lake access?
No — we bring the boat and travel to you. You just need a lake. If you're at a friend's cottage or a rental, that works too. For locations more than 1.5hrs from Manotick a distance surcharge applies.
What age can kids start?
Kids 6 and older are welcome. Children under 16 must be with a parent or guardian. We have equipment in all sizes and lots of experience coaching kids — the boom makes learning safe and fast for young riders.
What if the weather is bad?
Light rain — we go. Heavy rain or thunder forecasted — we reschedule. Christian monitors conditions the night before and will contact you early if a reschedule is needed. You get a full credit to rebook, or a full refund if no date is available before September 30.
Can we try multiple sports in one session?
Absolutely. Full-day sessions often mix disciplines — waterski in the morning, wakeboard or wakesurf in the afternoon. Just mention your interests when booking and Christian will plan around them.
What's the deposit and payment process?
Once confirmed, a 30% deposit is due via Square to hold your date. Full payment is due 48 hours before your session. Deposits are non-refundable but credited toward rebooking. See our refund policy for full details.
