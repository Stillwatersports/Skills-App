Booking & Pricing
A 30% deposit is required to secure your date — no date is held without it. Full payment is due 48 hours before your session via Square payment link. No payment is required just to submit a booking request.
Everything. The boat, fuel, all equipment (skis, boards, ropes, handles, life jackets in all sizes), and coach time. The only additional costs are distance surcharges (if applicable) and a $30/hr tubing surcharge. No hidden fees.
The 30% deposit is non-refundable in case of cancellation by the client. If Stillwater Sports cancels for non-weather reasons, you receive a full reschedule or refund. Cancellations must be submitted by email to bookings@learnwatersports.ca — not accepted by phone or text. See our full refund policy.
Light rain — we go. Heavy rain or thunder — we reschedule with full credit. Overcast or wind only — session proceeds; if conditions deteriorate, you receive a pro-rated credit for remaining time. If no suitable rebook date exists before September 30, you receive a full refund. The coach has final say on all weather decisions.
Yes — multi-day packages offer meaningful savings. Options include Double Weekday, 3 Day Work Week, Full Weekend, Holiday Weekend, and 5 Day Week packages. See the pricing page for full details. Consecutive day bookings get the best rate per day.
When you book a Single Session ($600), you receive a 10% credit toward your next future booking. It's our way of rewarding clients who come back. The credit applies to any future package.
Location & Logistics
No boat required — we bring our own. You need access to a body of water that allows motorized boats and a usable boat launch on the same waterway. We typically go to our clients' cottage, but we also operate on the Rideau River in Ottawa and can arrange sessions at public parks or launches if needed.
A concrete or gravel trailer ramp is ideal. We arrive with a full-size ski boat and trailer, so the launch must accommodate both, with parking for the truck and trailer. Any culverts, bridges, or locks on the route require a minimum 8 feet of clearance. If you're unsure, send us a description and we'll confirm.
We travel across Ontario cottage country — Ottawa, Muskoka, Kawarthas, Rideau Lakes, Prince Edward County, Thousand Islands, and beyond. Distance surcharges apply: +$100 flat for 1.5–3hr drives, +$200 flat for 3–6hr drives from Ottawa. For locations further than 6hrs, contact us and we'll see what we can do.
A minimum of 8 feet of clearance is required for any culverts, bridges, or locks on the route to the boat launch. Please measure and advise us before booking if there are any obstructions on the route.
Participants & Safety
All ages are welcome — our youngest skier to date was 2.5 years old, our oldest is… undisclosed! We have equipment in all sizes. Children under 16 must have a parent or guardian present on shore at all times.
Basic swimming ability is required for all participants entering the water. All riders wear a properly fitting PFD at all times in the water — safety is our top priority. If you have concerns, contact us before booking.
Groups of all sizes are welcome. The Sanger DLX accommodates up to 8 passengers comfortably. As a general guideline, plan for approximately 30 minutes per rider per session including setup — 1–2 turns each is plenty. Large groups can rotate riders throughout the day. There's no extra charge per person.
Yes — a signed liability waiver is required for every participant before entering the boat or water. Waivers for minors must be signed by a parent or legal guardian. Waivers will be sent upon booking confirmation. If a minor's parents will not be present, additional written consent is required in advance.
Absolutely. We provide everything, but if you have a favourite lifejacket, ski, handle, or rope — bring it. A well-fitting lifejacket in particular makes a noticeable difference and we always encourage clients to bring their own if they have one.
Alcohol on the boat is prohibited by law. Participants who have consumed alcohol in excess will not be permitted to ride — no refund will be issued. That said, a drink on shore is totally fine, and the coach may even join you for one after the session wraps up.
